## More Than Aesthetics

Colour is the first thing a consumer registers — before the logo, before the headline, before a single word is read. Research suggests that up to 90% of snap judgements about products are based on colour alone. Yet many brands treat their palette as an afterthought.

Understanding the psychology behind chromatic choice isn't about following a simplistic chart of "blue equals trust." It's about understanding cultural context, competitive positioning, and emotional resonance.

Warm colours (reds, oranges, yellows) tend to increase heart rate and create urgency. Cool colours (blues, greens, purples) have a calming effect and build feelings of stability.

### Context Changes Everything

- **Red** signals urgency in a sale banner but warmth on a restaurant logo
- **Black** reads as luxury for fashion but as mourning in certain cultures
- **Green** implies sustainability for a food brand but financial growth for a fintech
- **White** space communicates premium simplicity or clinical sterility depending on context
